Lisa Hines

Teacher, Counsellor, and Learner

I was born in a tiny village in Africa, but grew up in Canada, interacting with international workers throughout my childhood.

I graduated from the University of Waterloo with a Bachelor of Math in Computer Science and the Math Teaching Option, and Queen's University with a Bachelor in Education.

My first teaching gig was at St. Mildred's-Lightbourn School in Oakville, ON. There, I experienced the joy of introducing girls to robotics and building confidence in mathematics.

After 3 years, I moved to Cambodia to teach at Logos International School. Logos is an incredible place in the middle of a developing nation, where students are able to obtain a Western-standard education from a Christian worldview. The staff and students at Logos are very special.

Following Cambodia, I moved to Toronto to pursue a Masters degree at Tyndale Seminary. I am currently studying Clinical Counselling. My counselling interests include third-culture individuals, sexuality issues, and working with young adults. I also love studying biblical Hebrew along the way!

On the side, I teach children Robotics and Programming at Focus Learning and tutor Calculus and Statistics at Tyndale University College. I try to keep my toes in both programming and math on the side.

When I am not busy teaching or studying, I'm often found at Clarkson Community Church, swimming, biking, or hanging out with my two nephews and one niece. I love learning more about what community means and how to love others better.
